Hostels Students’ residential necessities are paramount for the management of St Aloysius College. The college has separate student hostels in each of the campuses for both men and lady students. Furthermore, the hostels in the main campus are divided into hostels for undergraduate and postgraduate students. Both ladies and gents hostels are headed by directors. In addition, the ladies hostels are managed by Sisters of Charity, ably led by a warden from the sister’s ranks. Hostel Seats for Socially and economically weaker sections: 50% of the hostel seats are reserved for students from socially and economically weaker sections In addition to the on-campus hostels, off-campus hostels are in the plans for starting residential programmes. It is proposed to have understanding with private operators for immediate needs. College Hostel was established in 1907: what was then a small venture to provide accommodation in the campus for faraway students is now enormously grown institution with 600 students from various states and countries. Living in common the Hostel promotes academic excellence, multi-faceted personality and invaluable unity in diversity. St Aloysius College Gents Hostel is essentially a place meant for PU, Degree and PG students who want to build an unique personality which is physically fit, socially responsible, spiritually nourishing and academically excellent.
